## Progression of Ideas Across Chapters

### 1) Setup (Chapter 1)
- `FARMER.md` premise (farmer + load + urgency) is concentrated here.
- The chapter plants the controlling misconception: faster-looking means better.

### 2) Escalation by Compounding Choices (Chapters 2-3)
- The outline uses a repeatable pattern:
  - attempt quick fix,
  - create mismatch,
  - increase friction and delay.
- This keeps the parable rhythm while preserving clear chapter focus.

### 3) False Solution and Thematic Clarification (Chapters 4-5)
- Circus horses embody a key insight: obedience and performance are context-specific.
- The comic choreography (side-step, bow, parade trot) dramatizes wrong tool for job.

### 4) Consequence and Moral Pivot (Chapter 6)
- The market bell and final obstacle convert mishaps into a causal chain.
- This chapter positions Part 2 themes:
  - craft over speed,
  - leadership over shouting,
  - system over shortcuts.

## Theme-to-Chapter Coverage Check

Use this checklist while drafting:

- Impatience is visible, not just stated (Chapters 1-2)
- Each bad decision is distinct: team, route, and training context (Chapters 2, 3, 5)
- Consequences worsen each time (Chapter 2 < Chapter 3 < Chapter 5)
- Clock pressure is reintroduced before finale (Chapters 4-6)
- Moral is implied before being stated (Chapters 5-6)

## One-line Story Logic

A rushed farmer keeps substituting flashy fixes for compatible systems, and each mismatch turns speed-seeking into delay until the market deadline forces him to confront how work actually gets done.
