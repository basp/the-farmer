### Chapter 3: Bad Decision #2: The Twisty Lanes to Town

**Overview**
- **Title:** Bad Decision #2: The Twisty Lanes to Town
- **Perspective:** First-person (The Farmer)
- **Purpose:** Escalate chaos by choosing a shortcut through narrow, busy lanes.
- **Tone:** Frantic, comedic, increasingly out of control.

---

#### 1. The Shortcut Scheme
- **Scene:** Farmer, frustrated by the last mishap, decides the “quickest” way is through the town’s twisty, crowded lanes.
- **Rationale:** “A straight line is fastest—if you ignore the bumps!”

#### 2. Into the Maze
- **Action:** The mismatched team enters the lanes. Describe the sudden noise, tight turns, and bustling market-day crowds.
- **Obstacles:** Geese, barrows, shouting vendors, clanging tinsmith, children darting.

#### 3. The Team Unravels
- **Conflict:** Thunderbolt spooks at a clanging pan; Barnaby and Blue try to hold steady. The farmer’s commands become frantic.
- **Comedic Imagery:** “Like threading a needle with a pitchfork.”

#### 4. The Shortcut Becomes a Delay
- **Mishap:** Every shortcut leads to a new obstacle or delay. The wagon gets wedged, a wheel bumps a barrow, produce nearly spills.
- **Running Gag:** Farmer insists, “Still faster!” as the clock ticks.

#### 5. Public Spectacle
- **Consequence:** Children laugh, adults duck, the farmer’s pride is dented. The horses are frazzled.
- **Insight:** A bystander quips, “Shortcuts are long when you’re in a hurry, neighbor!”

#### 6. The Outskirts Truce
- **Transition:** The chaos finally subsides as they reach the town’s edge, setting up the next bad decision.

---

**Key Vocabulary & Context**
- “Shortcuts,” “spooking,” “market-day,” “barrow,” “wedged,” “commands.”

**Narrative Job Check**
- Show escalation of chaos, compounding of earlier mistakes, and foreshadow the next “solution.”
