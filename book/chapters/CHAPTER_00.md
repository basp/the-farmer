### Chapter 0: The View from the Field (Before the Bell)

The predawn mist hung thick over the low-lying fields of Muddy Gate Farm, a silver-gray blanket that clung to the horses’ fetlocks and muffled the world. On the quiet outskirts of town, the only sounds were the rhythmic, slow munching of grass and the deep, synchronized breathing of Barnaby and Blue.

The two cold-bloods stood as a single unit of muscle and memory. Barnaby gave a low, vibrating nicker, answered by a soft nudge from Blue’s muzzle. For them, the coming day wasn't a race or a burden; it was a steady dance they had performed a thousand times. They felt the weight of the work not as a struggle, but as a purpose—a familiar, shared rhythm of pull and release.

Then, a single yellow light flickered in the farmhouse window.

The horses shifted, their heavy frames tensing. The "quiet" was over; the "loud" was coming. From within the house came the familiar clatter of boots and the muffled, hurried curses of the Fidgety One. He hadn't always been so brittle. When he first brought Barnaby and Blue to these fields, he was a springy lad with an optimistic whistle and a loose-handed grip. But years of chasing the clock and "optimizing" the impossible had worn him down to a bundle of nerves. He was already behind his own mental schedule, his mind spinning with shortcuts.

The farmhouse door creaked open, slicing through the stillness. The farmer approached, his silhouette heavy with more than just his usual gear. Barnaby’s ear flicked, catching the unfamiliar, sharp clink of a third harness—a spare set of leather that had no business on a two-horse team. Blue nudged his partner, a silent, puzzled question. They only ever needed two.

As the Fidgety One neared, his breath blooming like white clouds in the cold morning air, the horses stood ready. The peace of the field was about to meet the friction of human impatience.
