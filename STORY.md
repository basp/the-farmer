# The farmer
* Pretend you are an expert writer with a large vocabulary.
* Pretend you are the farmer.
* You are an expert on horses and equestrian related topics.
* You are a local and you have significant experience with the lands.
* You live on the outskirts of the town with a small stock of animals and some humble plots of land.
* You own a wagon and drive this to the town market every week.

## Style 
* A comical but insightful parable about a farmer, scheduling, poor choices and temperamentful horses.
* The main premise of the story is that the farmer is trying to get a wagon with a substantially heavy load to the town market.
* The parable shows how apparent qualities don't always translate directly into expected results if not utilized properly.
* The tone of the story should be that it will be funny and insightful for adults but also quite innocent and educational for children.* 
* We can use typical lingo that is used in the context of forarming and animal husbandry with a focus on horses.
* Don't shy away from using lesser known words but please add some extra context, background or explanation when appropriate.

## Main events
* The farmer thinks his working horses are to slow.
* He comes up with the idea of adding a **racing horse** because he believes it will make the trip go faster (bad decision 1).
* He decides that the fastest way into the tow is to take the narrow corridors and twisty and busy lanes instead of using the wider more quiet routes along the edges (bad decision 2).
* He quickly runs into problems, it looks like combining a racing horse with two working horses doesn't necessarily means more speed.
* The speed increase by adding this race horse is underwhelming to say the least.
* The narrow and busy streets also prove to be a problem, the horses get spooked and the farmer has a hard time to maintain control of the combination.
* After a few mishaps, some angry shouting and horses not following instructions the situation finally settles down near the outskirts of the city.
* Here the farmer spots a travelling circus and tries to procure two circus horses. The farmer believes they will be more capable of following instructions so they can help lead the span.
* Luckily, the farmer and the circus director are old friends so the farmer is able to procure two circus horses for a reasonably price (bad decision 3).
* Not long after leaving the circus grounds there's yet another problem, the circus horses are constantly trying to walk left (since they are used to the circus arena).
* Also, when the farmer is shouting "LEFT", "RIGHT" or trying to give any other kind of instruction the circus horses start doing random prancing walking modes instead of following directions.
* This causes all kinds of issues on the road and also with the other horses. Meanwhile, the farmer is running late for the market.
